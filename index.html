About Me

I am a Physics and Mathematics double major with a passion for understanding the fundamental laws of the universe through computational methods.

Currently, I apply machine learning techniques to investigate Weyl points in B20 compounds and perform Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations. My goal is to pivot these computational skills toward High Energy Particle Physics, contributing to research at major facilities like CERN or Fermilab.

Current Research & Projects

Computational Materials Science

Utilizing Python and VASP to analyze B20 chiral compounds. Implementing machine learning descriptors to predict electronic properties and topological features.

Antimatter detector

In an intersection of experiment and computation, we use monte carlo simulations to predict the angular distrubition of muons and anti-muons as they pass through a magnetic field. We only have 1 detector, so it is critical that we choose our detector placement carefully. We then use the Scikit-HEP python package to aid in data-analysis.
