About Me

I am a Physics and Mathematics double major driven by the challenge of modeling complex physical systems. My work focuses on the intersection of theoretical physics and advanced computation.

Currently, I am investigating Topological Weyl Semimetals using a custom Machine Learning pipeline to screen thousands of grain boundary interfaces. My broader ambition is to pivot these high-performance computing skills toward High Energy Particle Physics, contributing to experimental analysis at major facilities like CERN or Fermilab.

Current Research & Projects

ML-Driven Discovery in Chiral Magnets

Investigating how grain boundary geometry affects Weyl points and DMI in B20 CoGe. I developed a Python pipeline to analyze ~13,000 interface structures using physics-aware SOAP descriptors. By leveraging UMAP and HDBSCAN for unsupervised clustering, I identified representative candidates for high-fidelity VASP DFT calculations, effectively bridging Big Data with Quantum Mechanics.

Python (ASE/DScribe) Unsupervised ML VASP / DFT Topological Physics

Antimatter Detector Simulation

At the intersection of experiment and computation, I utilize Monte Carlo simulations to predict the angular distribution of muons and anti-muons passing through a magnetic field. Optimizing the placement of our single detector is critical; I use the Scikit-HEP ecosystem and ROOT to analyze simulation data and maximize detection efficiency.

C++ / ROOT Monte Carlo Scikit-HEP Experiment Design
